Chives

Welcome to Chives, a restaurant located just outside of Green Bay, known for its extravagant dishes and as a favorite among numerous players, most notably Aaron Rodgers. Chives is renowned for its excellent cuts of meat and fish.

As I approached Chives, one thing I really enjoyed was the ambiance of the restaurant. Chives is located in an old historic building in Suamico. Inside, it was bustling with a slightly casual but elegant feel. As you walk in, you’ll notice a long bar where you can enjoy a drink while you wait for your table. If you want to skip the wait, I highly recommend making a reservation online, as Chives has rather limited seating.

As we were seated at our table and browsed the menu, one unique aspect of Chives stood out: they keep their menu relatively limited. This allows them to serve dishes with the freshest, highest quality ingredients available. Their menu is broken down into three tiers based on portion sizes: Small plates, Medium plates, and Large plates.

After mulling over the menu, I opted for the Duroc Pork Porterhouse. At the time of my visit, it was priced at $26 for a 14oz pork porterhouse. The porterhouse was accompanied by two sides: Brussels sprouts and roasted potatoes. It was topped with a whole grain cream sauce.

As for the food – the pork chop was great. It was beautifully seasoned and had great flavor. However, the sides were lacking. As you can see from my photo above, the Brussels sprouts seemed quite plain. Maybe it’s my personal preference, but I usually enjoy a bit of color on my Brussels sprouts. As for the roasted potatoes, the few I was served were just okay. For $26, it was slightly disappointing to have half the dish be so lackluster.

All in all, there were a lot of great things at Chives, and I would happily go back. However, I don’t think it will ever be my go-to place or a spot I’m quick to recommend when people ask where they should dine. That being said, I would definitely visit Chives again.
