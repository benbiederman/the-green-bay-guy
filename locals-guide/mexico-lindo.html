Mexico Lindo

Welcome to Mexico Lindo, a Mexican restaurant in Green Bay that has been serving the community since 2020. As a big fan of Mexican food, I was excited to try another spot in the Green Bay area.

Mexico Lindo is located in a shared building with several other businesses, so parking can be a bit tight, depending on how busy the other businesses are. However, this shouldn’t impact parking too much - you might just have to walk a bit farther to reach Mexico Lindo, depending on the time of day.

Upon entering Mexico Lindo, I was surprised by the size of the restaurant’s seating area. Along with their pleasant outdoor patio, they offer several rooms with ample seating for those looking to enjoy a meal at Mexico Lindo. Thanks to the spacious layout, we didn’t have to wait for a table.

The menu at Mexico Lindo is quite extensive, with plenty of options for everyone. After browsing, I decided to try the Fajita Burrito - a burrito filled with grilled chicken, corn, shredded cheese, rice, and black beans, all covered in chipotle cheese dip.

While we waited for our food, a server approached us with a cart offering freshly made guacamole. If we had chosen to order some, the server would prepare it at our table, allowing us to customize it to our taste. Although we opted not to have any this time, I thought this was a great touch.

After a short wait, our food arrived. I have to be honest: the Fajita Burrito didn’t look very appetizing at first. However, it tasted better than it looked. While the burrito was decent, I was somewhat disappointed. I expected more of a traditional burrito rather than a rolled-up tortilla with ingredients falling out of both ends. It might just be the style of this burrito. The flavor was okay - but ultimately, I'd describe the burrito as very forgettable. My wife, who ordered the enchiladas, spoke very highly of her meal, so I might have just chosen an off dish from the menu.

The total for our meal, which included the Fajita Burrito, cheese enchiladas, and a side of rice and beans, came to $41. I thought it was a bit pricey for what we got, though not excessively so.

Overall, I would consider returning to Mexico Lindo, but I don’t think I’d order the Fajita Burrito again. While it wouldn’t be my top choice among Mexican restaurants in town, it’s worth a visit. For now, I’d probably opt for La Caretta or El Sarape.
