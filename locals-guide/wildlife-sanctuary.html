Wildlife Sanctuary

If you’re looking to explore and enjoy the outdoors while supporting a great cause, the Wildlife Sanctuary is the place to go. With over 500 acres of refuge for numerous animals, the Wildlife Sanctuary offers visitors the chance to explore the grounds and interact with various species.

With such a large campus—over 500 acres—there's plenty to do for people who are visiting. The sanctuary features hiking trails open daily that span the entire campus, so bring your walking shoes and plan to enjoy the outdoors as you walk the trails between the buildings.

Speaking of buildings, each one on the campus offers visitors something unique. One building is the Nature Center, which gives visitors a chance to see wolves, deer, and other animals that are being rehabilitated. Another is the observation building, which overlooks a scenic pond and features a feeding area. If you’re planning on feeding the birds, be sure to bring some cash, as corn is sold for $2 per bag.

Even though admission is free, I highly recommend bringing a little extra cash to make a donation to the Wildlife Sanctuary. The sanctuary has many expenses related to maintaining the grounds and the animal rehabilitation work they do, so even a small donation can go a long way.

I really can’t recommend the Wildlife Sanctuary enough, especially if you’re a nature lover or visiting the area with kids. The trails are wonderful to explore, and it’s truly rewarding to see all the good the Wildlife Sanctuary is doing for the animals in the area.
