Green Bay Packers Ticket Guide

Acquiring tickets for a Green Bay Packers game at Lambeau Field can be a formidable task. With a waiting list boasting over 130,000 eager fans, Packers season tickets are a cherished privilege reserved for those who hail from generations of Packers enthusiasts. Thanks to this extensive waiting list and the unwavering devotion of die-hard fans, Lambeau Field has consistently sold out for every Packers game since 1960. With the Packers returning to their winning ways in the early 90s and maintaining their excellence, the demand for tickets to a Packers game at Lambeau Field has reached unprecedented levels.

"Can I even secure tickets to a game?"

Certainly. Anyone can obtain a ticket to a Packers game at Lambeau Field, provided they have the financial means to support it. While tickets can be quite pricey, several factors can influence their cost. If you're aiming to attend a Packers game without breaking the bank, consider these factors that significantly affect ticket prices:

Opponent: The ticket prices can vary based on the Packers' opponent. Games against NFC North rivals or other NFC powerhouses like the Cowboys, Seahawks, or Falcons tend to command higher prices due to the heightened demand to witness these clashes. Additionally, if the Packers face a team with a fervent fan base, such as the Steelers, ticket prices can rise due to increased demand.

Packers' Record: Although the Packers have maintained a sold-out status since 1960, acquiring Packer tickets becomes more feasible during less successful seasons. For instance, in 2017, when Aaron Rodgers was sidelined with a collarbone injury, ticket holders were eager to sell their tickets. Prior to the Monday Night game against the Lions, some "fans" were selling their tickets for as low as $20 each. Conversely, after Super Bowl XLV, ticket prices surged, making them harder to find at reasonable prices. Fans tend to flock to games where their team is expected to win.

Time of Year: Lambeau Field's design exposes fans to challenging weather conditions. As winter approaches, typically around December, ticket availability can improve, depending on the opponent and the team's performance. Some season ticket holders may sell their tickets when the weather becomes too harsh for their liking. Preseason games also offer an opportunity for budget-friendly tickets, with prices often as low as $25-30 per ticket. While preseason games may not provide the same experience as regular-season games, they can be a cost-effective way to enjoy Lambeau Field, especially for families with young children. In contrast, playoff games see increased demand regardless of weather conditions, as they represent a win-or-eliminate scenario.

Time of Purchase: Taking the risk of purchasing tickets close to game day can yield significant savings. Keep an eye on ticket prices as the game approaches. Prices tend to drop as kickoff time nears, with season ticket holders lowering their prices in hopes of selling their tickets.
