Graystone Ale House

Discover Graystone Ale House, a bar and restaurant nestled in the De Pere, Wisconsin. Since 2010, Graystone has been serving locals with a diverse array of delectable dishes. Curious to experience it for myself, I ventured to this spot.

Arriving on an early Friday evening, the place was already bustling with activity. Thankfully, we were promptly seated by a warm and friendly waitress who truly made the experience enjoyable. Genuine hospitality can be rare to find, but she exceeded expectations.

Interestingly, Graystone Ale House shares ownership with The Bar and Hagemeister Park, which left me somewhat undecided about my feelings towards it. While I've had mostly negative experiences at Hagemeister Park, I always found The Bar to be decent. Nevertheless, I wanted to see what Graystone had to offer on its own merit.

For my dinner, I opted for a classic cheeseburger accompanied by tater tots (who says I can't enjoy some childhood nostalgia?). The tater tots portion was pretty sad. The burger was okay, but overall, the entire meal lacked anything to make it memorable.

On the positive side, Graystone Ale House offers a convenient shuttle service to Lambeau Field, a significant draw for those visiting Green Bay during Packers games. Such thoughtful amenities can truly enhance the overall experience.

In conclusion, Graystone Ale House was just okay. I wouldn't mind returning, however, given the plethora of other exciting dining options in the area, it may be a while before my next visit. Should you find yourself in the Green Bay vicinity, Graystone is worth a try, but there's no shortage of more appealing places to explore.
