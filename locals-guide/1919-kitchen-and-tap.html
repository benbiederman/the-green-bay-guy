1919 Kitchen and Tap

Located inside the beautiful Lambeau Field, 1919 Kitchen and Tap offers a refreshing change from Curly's, with a more updated look and feel. The restaurant features a massive bar with TVs at every angle, making it the perfect place to watch the game while enjoying some of Wisconsin's finest craft beers. However, when it comes to the food, my experiences have been mixed. While their cheese curds are absolutely amazing, the rest of the menu has often disappointed with cold dishes and long wait times. Considering the high prices, it's not a place I would frequently visit for dining.

The atmosphere at 1919 Kitchen & Tap is one of its biggest strengths. Unlike Curly's Pub, it feels like a natural extension of Lambeau Field. The design complements the stadium beautifully, and the restaurant offers a stunning view of the 50-foot Lombardi trophy. The extensive drink menu, particularly the craft beers, ensures you'll have plenty of options to enjoy while watching sports on one of the many TVs.

Despite the appealing ambiance and excellent drink selection, the food quality has been inconsistent. On multiple visits, I've encountered issues such as incorrect orders and poor-quality dishes. Additionally, as a popular tourist spot, the prices tend to be on the higher side.

Overall, 1919 Kitchen & Tap is a beautifully designed restaurant and an excellent spot for drinks, especially their top-notch cheese curds. However, for dining, I would recommend other local establishments like Rustique Pizzeria, or Copper State Brewing, which offer better food at more reasonable prices. Also, keep in mind that the restaurant can get extremely busy on game day weekends, given its prime location inside Lambeau Field.
