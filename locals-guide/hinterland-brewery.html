Hinterland Brewery

Located just across from Lambeau Field is Hinterland Brewery, a restaurant that relocated to the area with the addition of the Titletown District. Known for its vast selection of beers and a food menu offering a wide range of delicious options, Hinterland Brewery has been serving the Green Bay area since 1995.

If you’re planning to dine at Hinterland Brewery, be aware that parking can be a bit challenging. The parking lot is shared with The Turn, Titletown District, and Titletown Tech, so you may need to walk a little to reach the restaurant. However, it's not an unreasonable distance—just slightly farther than what you’d expect for a typical restaurant in Green Bay, depending on the time of day and what’s happening in the Titletown District.

While I’m usually not a fan of the more touristy restaurants in Green Bay, such as Kroll’s West and 1919 Kitchen and Tap, I decided to give Hinterland Brewery a try to see how it stacks up. The first thing that caught our attention upon entering was how impressive the building is. It has a distinctly industrial feel, with their brewery equipment proudly displayed through large windows. The seating area is quite spacious, with the option for outdoor dining as well.

We chose to sit outside since it was one of the rare beautiful weather days we get in Green Bay. We were promptly seated and asked to scan a QR code for the menu. After some deliberation, we decided on the Smoked BBQ Pulled Pork sandwich, a wood-fired pizza, and chicken tenders for our little one. My wife also tried one of the sour beers from their selection.

After a short wait, our food arrived. As someone who typically avoids restaurants near Lambeau Field due to past poor experiences, I was pleasantly surprised. The pizza had an excellent crust—chewy with a nice crunch—and the sauce was fresh without being overpowering. The Smoked BBQ Pulled Pork sandwich was piled high and perfectly balanced with the cherry wheat BBQ sauce. And as a fan of chicken tenders, I was happy to discover that the ones in our son’s meal were great—luckily, he’s only one, so I got to sample one!

The only downside of our experience was that our waitress seemed overloaded. It appeared she had too many tables in her section, and as a result, the wait to get our check was quite long. Normally, it wouldn’t have been an issue, but a one-year-old can only be patient for so long. Eventually, my wife took our son to the park in the Titletown District while I waited to settle the bill. Our total came to $66, which is a bit pricier than most Green Bay restaurants with similar menus, though the location likely plays a role in that.

Overall, the food exceeded my expectations, especially given the proximity to Lambeau Field. However, I don’t see Hinterland Brewery becoming one of my regular spots, mainly due to the cost.
